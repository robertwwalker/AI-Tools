The Calculus of Chaos

Capacity Optimization & Stochastic Simulation for High-Stakes Systems

1. The General Service Problem

The General Service Problem states that total system cost is the sum of Capacity Costs (staffing) and Waiting Costs (patient suffering/lost revenue). Because arrival patterns follow a **Poisson Process**, they exhibit "clumping."

Variability causes queues to form even when average capacity exceeds average demand. We define the **Stability Criterion** as:

$$\rho = \frac{\lambda}{c \mu} < 1$$

If $\rho \geq 1$, the system is non-stationary; the queue will grow indefinitely over time. Our lab helps you find the "Elbow" where adding one more server provides the maximum reduction in volatility.

2. Mathematical Models & Recurrence

A. The M/M/c Derivation

The probability $P_0$ that all servers are idle is the foundation of the model:

$$P_0 = \left[ \sum_{n=0}^{c-1} \frac{(c\rho)^n}{n!} + \frac{(c\rho)^c}{c!(1-\rho)} \right]^{-1}$$

B. Circular (M/M/c/K) Finite Buffer

When capacity is limited to $K$ slots, the Blocking Probability ($P_B$) describes the frequency with which patients are diverted.

$$P_B = P_K = P_0 \frac{(c\rho)^K}{c! c^{K-c}}$$

C. Priority Triage Logic

Priority systems reduce wait times for Class 1 by shifting the "Variance Burden" to Class 2.

$$W_{class2} = \frac{E[S^2]\lambda}{2(1-\rho_1)(1-\rho_1-\rho_2)}$$

D. Deque (Reneging)

This models **Customer Churn**. A patient exits the queue (reneges) if their wait time $W$ exceeds their patience threshold $T$.

3. The Shock Theory

A System Shock is a transient period where $\lambda(t) \gg c\mu$. In these states, the "Average Wait" is a meaningless metric. We must instead calculate the Recovery Time required to clear the deterministic backlog:

$$T_{recovery} = \frac{Q_{backlog}}{c\mu - \lambda_{baseline}}$$
