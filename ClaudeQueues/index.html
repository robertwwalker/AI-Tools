Welcome to the Operations Science Laboratory

This interactive laboratory provides hands-on exploration of queueing theory and capacity optimization through browser-based stochastic simulation. All computation runs entirely in your browser using Python (via Pyodide) with no server dependencies.

Healthcare systems face a fundamental challenge: arrivals follow a Poisson process, exhibiting natural "clumping" that creates queues even when average capacity exceeds average demand. Traditional planning methods that rely on simple averages fail catastrophically in these environments, especially during surge events.

Through ensemble simulation (60+ parallel realities), we capture the full spectrum of operational volatility and identify optimal capacity configurations. Our time-path visualizations reveal system dynamics during normal operations and shock scenarios, showing exactly how long it takes systems to recover from surge events.

New: Shock Scenario Analysis — Each model now supports testing four surge magnitudes (baseline, 2×, 3×, 5× arrivals) with visual recovery analysis. This enables evidence-based surge capacity planning and preparedness assessment.

M/M/c Standard Queue

INFINITE CAPACITY MODEL

The foundational multi-server queue with Poisson arrivals and exponential service times. Assumes unlimited waiting capacity—the theoretical baseline for all queueing analysis.

🏥 Clinical Applications:
  • Emergency department physician staffing
  • Operating room scheduling optimization
  • Outpatient clinic capacity planning
  • Radiology service configuration

Key Insight: Utilization (ρ) must stay below 100% for stability. At 90% utilization, wait times become highly volatile. The "elbow" typically occurs around 75-80% utilization.

Finite Capacity (M/M/c/K)

BLOCKING PROBABILITY MODEL

Extends M/M/c with a maximum system capacity K. When K patients are in the system (served + waiting), new arrivals are rejected or diverted. Critical for resource-constrained environments.

🏥 Clinical Applications:
  • Inpatient bed management (fixed bed count)
  • ICU capacity with hard limits
  • Ambulance diversion protocols
  • Boarding and overflow management

Key Insight: Blocking probability (PB) rises exponentially near capacity. Operating at >90% of K creates cascading regional failures as diverted patients overwhelm neighboring facilities.

Priority Queue (Triage)

NON-PREEMPTIVE PRIORITY MODEL

Implements triage logic where high-priority patients receive preferential service. Does not interrupt service-in-progress (non-preemptive). Redistributes variance—reduces wait for critical, increases wait for stable.

🏥 Clinical Applications:
  • Emergency severity index (ESI) triage
  • Trauma activation protocols
  • STEMI/stroke fast-track pathways
  • Urgent vs. routine surgical scheduling

Key Insight: Priority systems don't create capacity—they shift variance. Low-priority wait times can explode to 10-20× high-priority waits at high utilization. Requires careful equity considerations.

Reneging/Abandonment

PATIENCE-LIMITED MODEL

Models customer abandonment when wait times exceed individual patience thresholds. Patients leave without service (LWBS), creating revenue loss and poor outcomes. Self-regulating but costly.

🏥 Clinical Applications:
  • Left without being seen (LWBS) rates
  • Walk-in clinic patient retention
  • Call center abandonment optimization
  • Online check-in dropout prevention

Key Insight: Abandonment creates a "pressure relief valve" that prevents infinite queues, but represents system failure. Each LWBS patient is lost revenue, poor outcomes, and potential litigation risk.
